About Science Lab Parties

At our core, we believe science should be exciting, hands-on, and unforgettable—especially for kids. That's why we've created a one-of-a-kind party experience where curiosity takes center stage and every guest becomes a junior scientist.

Our science parties are designed to spark wonder from the moment kids put on their lab coats and safety glasses. For an extra-special touch, we offer optional personalized photo ID badges with lanyards so each child truly feels like part of the lab team.

The highlight of every party is our thrilling Elephant Foam Demonstration—a safe, colorful, and high-energy experiment that introduces kids to the power of catalysts in a way they'll never forget. But the fun doesn't stop there. Every child gets to participate in their own Elephant Foam activity, giving them a hands-on chance to explore the reaction up close.

We also dive into the fascinating world of non-Newtonian fluids with your choice of a slime-making or oobleck activity. Kids mix, stretch, and experiment while learning why these materials behave so differently from everyday liquids and solids. Each child creates their own labeled slime jar to take home—perfect as a party favor and a memory of their scientific adventure.

Throughout the party, science fair–style posters help bring the learning to life, breaking down big ideas like catalysts and non-Newtonian fluids into fun, easy-to-understand concepts.

But we don't just stop at chemistry. Our creative STEM activities include building edible "molecules" using strawberries, marshmallows, toothpicks, and pipe cleaners—combining imagination, engineering, and a little bit of delicious fun. Kids can also decorate their own petri dish snacks, adding another layer of creativity (and tastiness) to the experience.

Every detail of our parties is thoughtfully designed to balance education and excitement. Whether kids are experimenting, building, or creating, they're learning through play—and having an absolute blast doing it.

We're here to turn birthdays and special events into unforgettable science adventures—where messes are encouraged, questions are celebrated, and every child leaves feeling like a scientist.

🧪 Elephant Toothpaste Experiment

How It Works

Hydrogen peroxide is a liquid that can break down into water and oxygen gas. Yeast contains a special enzyme called catalase. Catalase speeds up the breakdown of hydrogen peroxide. When oxygen gas forms, the bubbles get trapped in dish soap and create a lot of foam that looks like toothpaste big enough for an elephant!

What Happened

The yeast helped break down hydrogen peroxide into water and oxygen gas. The oxygen bubbles got trapped in the soap and made a lot of foam. This reaction is called a catalyzed chemical reaction.

Materials

  • Plastic bottle
  • 1/2 cup hydrogen peroxide (3%)
  • 1 tablespoon dry yeast
  • 3 tablespoons warm water
  • Dish soap
  • Food coloring
  • Funnel
  • Tray
