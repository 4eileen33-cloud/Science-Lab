Elephant Toothpaste Experiment

Science Explanation

The Elephant Toothpaste experiment is a popular science demonstration that illustrates an exothermic reaction. In this experiment, hydrogen peroxide breaks down into water and oxygen gas with the help of yeast or potassium iodide as a catalyst. The rapid release of oxygen creates a foamy eruption resembling toothpaste being squeezed from a tube, hence the name.

Materials List

  • 16 oz. hydrogen peroxide (6% solution)
  • 1 packet of yeast
  • Warm water
  • Dish soap
  • Food coloring (optional)
  • Safety goggles
  • Plastic bottle or container
  • Tray to catch overflow
